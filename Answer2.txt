HTML Entities:
HTML entities are special codes that represent reserved characters, symbols, or characters with specific
meanings in HTML.HTML entities are written using an ampersand (&) followed by a
specific code or name, and ending with a semicolon (;).

Commonly Used Entities:
1. &lt; less than; such as: <
2. &gt; greater than; such as: >
3. &reg; registered trademark; such as: ®	
4. &copy; copyright ;such as: ©
5. &quot; double quotation mark; such as: “
